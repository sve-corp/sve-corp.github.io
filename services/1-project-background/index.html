Project Background

Physical metrics that can be integrated into a user experience greatly aid the functionality and immersivity of a product. From this general statement stems the direction and motive of the product. Our metric data points that are collected include the heart rate of the user and breathing rate of the user. These two data points are collected via a separate electronic apparatus that communicates via bluetooth with a VR headset. The device is worn around the neck and has passive functionality that sends information periodically. The nature of VR for the end user has a wide range of case uses. The specifics on what the information is used for is up to the developer of VR application. There is an expectation that these data points will be used in the: entertainment sector, the information sector, health sector, industry specific applications, and many more.

Some of the potential use cases of a biofeedback device are described below:

  • Biofeedback systems play a pivotal role in improving the physical health of a person by providing feedback information that influences their lifestyle.
  • Biofeedback devices have applications in sports as well. The sportsperson in the loop uses the feedback information to improve their execution performance.
  • Stress can be combated through relaxation techniques which depend on the information provided by the biofeedback device. Involuntary functions like breathing and heart rate can be manipulated to make sure that the body responds positively to stressful situations.

  • By designing a system that integrates VR with a biofeedback device, we can create a power tool for mental health interventions. Tailored virtual experiences can be created to overcome stress related mental health issues.
