Engineering Standards

  • UL EN 60 950-1 This is a UL standard (predominantly in the United States of America) that provides standards against fire, electric shock or injury to a person who may come in normal contact with the device.

  • AS/NZS 60950.1 This is a similar standard to the UL EN 60850-1 in the US, but applicable for Australia and New Zealand. **

  • C22.2, No. 60950 This is a similar standard to the UL EN 60850-1 in the US, but applicable for European countries.

  • CE - Certification that meets EU standards for health, safety and environmental requirements for a product sold within the EU.

  • BIS certification - Similar standards to the UL requirements, but specific for India under the Bureau of Indian Standards (BIS)

  • USA - FCC For the United States of America this is a certification for electrical goods that maintain a radio frequency emission within limits.

  • IEC 60601-1, 3.63 - This is a medical device standard that comes into requirement when an electrical piece of equipment either gives or detects energy from a patient. Common items that fall under this umbrele are EEG monitors, ECG devices, and vital signs monitors. While this may not be necessary for the device, this standard, when met, may provide unrealized benefit later.

  • CCC - China Compulsory Certification - A standard that electrical components need to meet to be able to be sold in China.
